# Home Diagnostic Testing E-Commerce Documentation

## 1. Project Overview

This project is an e-commerce platform designed especially for selling home diagnostic testing products, self-test kits, health monitoring devices, and related consumables. Customers can browse products, purchase them online, use the device or kit at home, and access a digital self-test report from their user account.

The main business goal is to make selected health testing more affordable and convenient for customers by helping them perform suitable tests at home instead of depending only on high-cost diagnostic center visits.

Important positioning:

- The website sells products for home testing, screening, and personal health monitoring.
- The system should clearly explain that home-test results are not a replacement for professional medical advice.
- Reports generated by the website should be transparent about their source.
- Diagnostic center templates should only be used when the diagnostic center has authorized the business to use that name, branding, or format.

The platform is designed for three main groups:

- Customers who want to buy diagnostic products and manage their reports.
- Admin staff who manage products, orders, customers, report templates, and business operations.
- Partner diagnostic centers or healthcare partners who may provide approved report template formats or branding permission.

## 2. Business Purpose

Many customers avoid routine testing because diagnostic center fees can be expensive, time-consuming, or inconvenient. This platform creates a lower-cost alternative for appropriate at-home testing products.

Use:

- Customers can buy reliable home diagnostic products online.
- Customers can perform selected tests at home.
- Customers can generate a clean PDF report for personal records.
- Customers can share the report with doctors, family members, or caregivers when needed.
- The business can sell test kits, devices, strips, refills, and related products through a familiar e-commerce experience.

## 3. Important Trust and Compliance Principles

Because the project deals with health-related products and reports, the platform should be built around trust, transparency, and safety.

Use:

- Protects customers from misunderstanding test results.
- Reduces business risk from misleading health claims.
- Makes the platform more credible for healthcare partners.
- Helps customers understand when they should consult a doctor or diagnostic center.

Recommended platform wording:

- "Self-test report generated from your submitted home-test result."
- "This report is for personal record and screening support."
- "Consult a qualified healthcare professional for diagnosis, treatment, or serious symptoms."
- "Diagnostic center-branded templates are shown only for authorized partner centers."

## 4. Customer-Facing E-Commerce Features

### Homepage

The homepage introduces the diagnostic product store and highlights important product categories, featured devices, offers, educational banners, and trusted product sections.

Use:

- Helps customers understand that the website sells home testing products.
- Promotes popular devices and test kits.
- Makes the platform feel professional and trustworthy.
- Can highlight affordability, privacy, convenience, and fast delivery.

### Product Categories

Products can be organized into categories such as blood glucose monitoring, blood pressure devices, cholesterol test kits, uric acid test kits, pregnancy tests, urine test strips, thermometer devices, pulse oximeters, consumables, and refill strips.

Use:

- Helps customers find the correct type of test product quickly.
- Keeps medical and wellness products organized.
- Makes the website easier to scale as more products are added.

### Product Listing Pages

Customers can browse all diagnostic products or browse by category, offer, hot deal, flash sale, or search result.

Use:

- Allows comparison between similar devices or test kits.
- Makes prices, stock, and offers visible.
- Helps customers choose between starter kits, refills, and accessories.

### Product Details Page

Each product page should explain what the product is used for, what is included in the box, how many tests it supports, whether refills are required, and what type of result the customer can expect.

Use:

- Reduces confusion before purchase.
- Helps customers buy compatible strips or accessories.
- Sets realistic expectations about home-test use.
- Encourages safe and correct product use.

Recommended product information:

- Product name and images.
- Test type or use case.
- Device/kit contents.
- Number of tests included.
- Compatible strips or refills.
- Basic usage summary.
- Storage instructions.
- Expiry date or batch/lot information when available.
- Warning and consultation notice.
- Price, discount, and stock status.

### Search and Filtering

Customers can search for products by name, test type, device type, or category. Filters may include price, brand, test type, stock availability, and compatible consumables.

Use:

- Helps customers quickly find the right health product.
- Reduces accidental purchases of incompatible strips or accessories.
- Improves conversion for customers who already know the test they need.

### Product Comparison

A comparison feature can help customers compare similar diagnostic products side by side.

Use:

- Helps customers choose between devices.
- Highlights differences such as price, test count, refill cost, memory, speed, and included accessories.
- Reduces support questions before purchase.

### Shopping Cart

Customers can add diagnostic products to the cart, adjust quantities, remove items, and continue shopping.

Use:

- Keeps the buying process familiar and simple.
- Supports purchasing multiple test kits or refill strips together.
- Helps families or caregivers buy multiple items in one order.

### Checkout

Customers can enter delivery information, select shipping area, add order notes, and choose payment method.

Use:

- Makes the platform work like a normal e-commerce store.
- Allows customers to buy products without technical or medical complexity.
- Supports home delivery for test kits and devices.

### Payment Options

The platform can support cash on delivery and online payment options such as mobile banking or payment gateways.

Use:

- Gives customers payment flexibility.
- Supports local buying habits.
- Makes the store suitable for both first-time and repeat customers.

### Order Confirmation

After checkout, customers receive confirmation that their order has been placed.

Use:

- Builds customer confidence.
- Provides a record of the order.
- Reduces uncertainty after payment or cash-on-delivery checkout.

### Order Tracking

Customers can track their order by account, phone number, or invoice/order number.

Use:

- Reduces support calls.
- Helps customers know when the device or kit will arrive.
- Improves post-purchase trust.

## 5. User Panel Features

### User Account

Customers can create an account, log in, update profile details, change password, and view order history.

Use:

- Keeps customer purchases and reports organized.
- Makes repeat purchases easier.
- Allows customers to access previous report PDFs.

### My Purchased Devices / Test Kits

The user panel can show products the customer has purchased, including devices, test kits, refill strips, and accessories.

Use:

- Helps customers identify which device or kit they own.
- Makes it easier to generate reports only for purchased products.
- Supports repeat ordering of compatible refills.

### QR Code Scanning for Purchased Products

Each eligible diagnostic product or device can include a QR code. After purchase, the customer can scan the QR code from the user panel or mobile browser to open the related report-generation page.

Use:

- Confirms that the customer is using a purchased product.
- Makes report creation faster.
- Reduces confusion about which product/report type to select.
- Connects the physical product with the digital account.

Suggested QR flow:

1. Customer buys a home diagnostic product.
2. Product/device packaging includes a QR code.
3. Customer logs into the user panel.
4. Customer scans the QR code.
5. Website identifies the product, test type, and eligible report form.
6. Customer enters the test result values.
7. Customer selects an available authorized report template.
8. Customer downloads the generated PDF report.

### Self-Test Result Entry

After scanning the QR code, the customer can enter test values based on the device or kit result.

Use:

- Allows customers to convert home-test readings into a clean report.
- Keeps reports organized in one account.
- Makes the result easier to share with a doctor or caregiver.

Possible fields:

- Test date and time.
- Customer name, age, gender, and contact number.
- Test type.
- Device or kit name.
- Batch/lot number.
- Result value.
- Unit of measurement.
- Reference range, if applicable and approved.
- Notes or symptoms, if the business allows it.
- Disclaimer and confirmation checkbox.

### PDF Report Download

Customers can download a PDF report after entering their test result. The report can use a predefined format selected from the available report templates.

Use:

- Gives customers a professional-looking health record.
- Makes self-test results easier to store or print.
- Helps customers share results in a familiar report format.

Report source rule:

- If a diagnostic center has formally approved the template, the PDF may use that center's authorized branding and format.
- If no approval exists, the report should be branded as a self-test report from the platform, not as a diagnostic center report.

### Authorized Diagnostic Center Template Selection

Customers may select from approved report templates created by the admin. Each template can represent either the platform's own self-test report design or an authorized partner diagnostic center format.

Use:

- Gives customers a familiar report layout.
- Supports partner diagnostic center branding where permitted.
- Keeps the report generation system flexible for different test types.

Required trust controls:

- Show whether the template is "Platform Self-Test Template" or "Authorized Partner Template."
- Do not show unauthorized diagnostic center names or logos.
- Add a footer explaining whether the report is self-entered or partner-verified.

### Report History

The user panel can store generated reports for future download.

Use:

- Helps customers keep personal health records.
- Makes repeat monitoring easier.
- Allows customers to compare previous and current readings over time.

### Reorder Compatible Products

From the user panel, customers can reorder test strips, refill kits, or accessories linked to their purchased device.

Use:

- Encourages repeat sales.
- Helps customers avoid buying incompatible consumables.
- Improves convenience for long-term monitoring products.

### Health Reminder Notifications

The platform can offer optional reminders for repeat testing, refill purchases, or device maintenance.

Use:

- Encourages responsible product use.
- Helps customers remember routine monitoring.
- Increases repeat purchase opportunities.

Use:

### Brand Management

Admins can manage brands and manufacturers.

Use:

- Helps customers choose trusted device brands.

### Inventory Management

Admins can track stock, purchase price, selling price, and availability of products.

Use:

Use:

### Customer Management

Admins can view and manage customer accounts.

Use:

- Helps support customers with orders and report access.

### Diagnostic Center Template Management

Admins can create and manage report templates for diagnostic-style PDF reports.

Use:

- Allows the business to provide structured PDF reports.
- Supports multiple test types and report formats.
- Lets authorized partner diagnostic centers have dedicated report styles.
- Keeps report layouts editable without changing the website code.

Template types:

- Platform self-test template.
- Authorized partner diagnostic center template.
- Test-specific template, such as glucose, cholesterol, uric acid, pregnancy, urine test, or other supported products.

Template fields:

- Template name.
- Template type.
- Diagnostic center name, if authorized.
- Authorization status.

- Address and contact details.
- Report title.
- Test fields.
- Unit labels.
- Reference range fields.
- Footer note.
- Disclaimer text.
- Signature area, if approved.

- Active/inactive status.

### Report Form Builder

Admins can define which input fields are required for each test report.

Use:

- Makes each report match the test product.
- Prevents customers from entering incomplete information.
- Supports different report structures for different test types.

Example field controls:

- Text input.
- Number input.
- Date and time.
- Dropdown selection.
- Unit selection.
- Reference range.
- Normal/high/low flag.
- Customer declaration checkbox.

### Report Template Preview

Admins can preview how a PDF report will look before activating the template.

Use:

- Reduces mistakes in report formatting.
- Helps ensure branding and text are correct.
- Makes it easier to approve partner templates.

### Generated Report Management

Admins can view generated reports, search by customer, product, QR code, test type, or date, and download reports if needed for support.

Use:

- Helps support customers who lose a report.
- Gives the business visibility into report usage.
- Creates a record of generated self-test reports.

### QR Code Management

Admins can create, upload, assign, or manage QR codes for diagnostic products or device batches.

Use:

- Connects physical products to digital report generation.
- Helps prevent report access for non-purchased products.
- Supports batch-based product tracking.

QR code may identify:

- Product.
- Device model.
- Batch or lot number.
- Purchase/order ID.
- Eligible report templates.
- Expiry date or validity period.

### Report Verification Page

Each generated report can include a verification QR code or verification ID. Anyone who scans it can confirm that the PDF was generated by the platform.

Use:

- Helps prevent fake edited PDFs.
- Gives doctors or recipients a way to verify the report source.
- Improves trust in the platform.

Verification page should show:

- Report ID.
- Customer name or masked identity.
- Test type.
- Report generation date.
- Template type.
- Whether it is self-entered, platform-generated, or partner-authorized.

### Diagnostic Center Partner Management

Admins can manage partner diagnostic center records if the business has formal partnerships.

Use:

- Keeps partner information organized.
- Supports authorized templates.
- Helps separate platform-generated reports from partner-approved formats.

Partner fields:

- Center name.
- Address.
- Contact number.
- Logo.
- Authorization document or note.
- Allowed templates.
- Active/inactive status.

### Safety Disclaimer Management

Admins can manage standard disclaimers shown on product pages, checkout, result entry forms, and PDF reports.

Use:

- Ensures customers understand the limitations of home testing.
- Reduces risk of misuse.
- Keeps health messaging consistent across the platform.

### Payment, SMS, Courier, and Email Settings

Admins can configure payment gateways, SMS gateway, courier services, and email settings.

Use:

- Supports normal e-commerce operations.
- Allows customer notifications.
- Helps manage delivery and communication.

### Reports and Analytics

Admins can view order reports, stock reports, profit reports, and generated report usage.

Use:

- Helps management understand product demand.
- Shows which tests are most popular.
- Supports purchasing and marketing decisions.

Suggested diagnostic analytics:

- Most sold test products.
- Most generated report types.
- Repeat purchases of strips/refills.
- Low-stock diagnostic items.
- Product profitability.
- Customer reorder frequency.

## 7. Report Generation Workflow

### Customer Flow

1. Customer buys a diagnostic device or self-test kit.
2. Product is delivered to the customer.
3. Customer performs the test at home by following product instructions.
4. Customer logs into the user panel.
5. Customer scans the QR code on the device/kit.
6. Website opens the matching report form.
7. Customer enters result values.
8. Customer selects an available report template.
9. Website generates a PDF report.
10. Customer downloads or prints the report.

### Admin Flow

1. Admin creates diagnostic product.
2. Admin assigns allowed report type and QR settings.
3. Admin creates or approves report template.
4. Admin defines report fields and disclaimers.
5. Admin activates the template.
6. Customer generates report after purchase.
7. Admin can review generated report records if support is needed.

## 8. Report Template Use Cases

### Platform Self-Test Report

This is the default and safest report type. It is branded with the platform name and clearly states that the result was entered by the customer from a home test.

Use:

- Best for general home-testing products.
- Transparent and trust-friendly.
- Suitable when no diagnostic center partnership exists.

### Authorized Diagnostic Center Template

This template may use a diagnostic center's name, logo, and format only if the center has approved it.

Use:

- Supports official partnerships.
- Makes reports familiar to customers.
- Helps the business build healthcare credibility.

### Partner-Verified Report

If the business later adds a service where a partner diagnostic center reviews or verifies the submitted result, the report can show partner verification status.

Use:

- Adds more trust than self-entered reports.
- Could become a premium service.
- Helps customers who need stronger credibility.

## 9. Safety and Customer Education Features

### Product Usage Instructions

Each diagnostic product should include basic usage guidance and a link to the official manual or instruction sheet.

Use:

- Helps customers use products correctly.
- Reduces invalid results caused by misuse.
- Improves customer satisfaction.

### Result Interpretation Notice

The website should avoid giving strong medical conclusions unless reviewed and approved by qualified professionals.

Use:

- Prevents customers from making unsafe medical decisions.
- Keeps the platform focused on screening and recordkeeping.
- Encourages professional consultation when needed.

### Doctor Consultation Reminder

Reports and result pages should remind customers to consult a doctor for symptoms, abnormal values, repeated abnormal results, pregnancy-related concerns, chronic disease decisions, or treatment changes.

Use:

- Protects customer health.
- Reduces misuse of home-test results.
- Builds responsible brand trust.

### Expiry and Lot Awareness

The system can remind customers to check expiry dates and lot numbers before using test kits or strips.

Use:

- Helps prevent inaccurate results from expired products.
- Supports product quality tracking.
- Gives customers better confidence in the test process.

## 11. Security and Privacy Features

### Secure User Access

Customers need to log in to access purchased products and reports.

Use:

- Protects personal report data.
- Prevents unauthorized report generation.
- Keeps customer history private.

### Admin Role and Permission Management

Admin users can have different roles and access levels.

Use:

- Restricts sensitive report template and customer data features.
- Allows staff to work safely in assigned areas.
- Protects business operations.

### Report Data Privacy

Health-related report information should be treated as sensitive customer data.

Use:

- Builds customer trust.
- Reduces risk from unauthorized access.
- Supports responsible handling of personal health information.

Recommended privacy controls:

- Limit who can view generated reports.
- Record report generation date and user.
- Avoid showing full health data in public verification pages.
- Allow customers to download their own reports from their account.

## 16. Final Positioning Statement

This platform should be presented as a home diagnostic product e-commerce system with self-test report generation. The strongest and safest market message is not that the website replaces diagnostic centers, but that it helps customers perform suitable basic tests at home, save money where appropriate, keep organized PDF records, and consult healthcare professionals when needed.

The diagnostic center template feature should be used only for authorized partner templates or clearly labeled self-test templates. This keeps the product useful, trustworthy, and ready for real-world business use.
