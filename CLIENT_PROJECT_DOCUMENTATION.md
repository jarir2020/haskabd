# E-Commerce Website Project Documentation

## 1. Project Overview

This project is a complete online shopping website designed for selling physical products through a public storefront and managing the business from a private admin panel. The website supports product display, customer ordering, payment handling, delivery coordination, inventory tracking, marketing campaigns, customer communication, and business reporting.

The system is built for three main groups:

- Customers who browse products, place orders, track orders, and manage their profiles.
- Business/admin staff who manage products, orders, customers, content, payments, delivery, and reports.
- Marketing and management teams who need promotional tools, performance information, customer trust elements, and sales visibility.

The goal of the website is to make online selling easier, reduce manual order handling, improve customer confidence, and give the business better control over day-to-day operations.

## 2. Customer-Facing Website Features

### Homepage

The homepage works as the main sales entry point for visitors. It can show banners, product categories, hot deals, flash sales, campaign promotions, customer review images, and product sections.

Use:

- Promotes the most important products and offers.
- Helps customers quickly find categories and popular items.
- Creates a strong first impression for the brand.
- Allows the business to highlight campaigns, discounts, and featured products.

### Product Categories

Products can be organized into categories, subcategories, and child categories. This creates a clear shopping structure for customers.

Use:

- Helps customers browse large product collections easily.
- Keeps the store organized as the product catalog grows.
- Allows the business to promote specific product groups.
- Improves the customer journey by reducing confusion.

### Product Listing Pages

Customers can view products by category, subcategory, child category, hot deals, flash sales, search results, or the full shop page. Product lists include price, previous price, stock information, and product images.

Use:

- Gives customers a simple way to compare products.
- Supports browsing by need, offer type, or product group.
- Makes discounts and stock availability more visible.
- Encourages product discovery beyond a single item.

### Sorting and Price Filtering

Customers can sort products by newest, oldest, price high-to-low, price low-to-high, and name. They can also filter products within a selected price range.

Use:

- Helps customers find products that match their budget.
- Makes shopping faster for customers who know what they want.
- Supports different buying behaviors, such as bargain hunting or premium product browsing.

### Search and Live Search

The website includes product search and live search suggestions. Customers can search by keyword and optionally narrow results by category.

Use:

- Helps customers quickly find a specific product.
- Reduces browsing time.
- Improves the chance of conversion when customers already know what they want.

### Product Details Page

Each product has a dedicated details page showing images, price, old price, description, stock, available sizes or colors, product unit, video, reviews, shipping options, and related products.

Use:

- Gives customers the information they need before buying.
- Supports products with variants such as size, color, or unit.
- Builds trust through reviews, images, and detailed descriptions.
- Encourages additional purchases through related products.

### Quick View

Customers can preview product information without fully leaving the current browsing page.

Use:

- Speeds up product comparison.
- Keeps customers engaged while browsing.
- Reduces friction before adding a product to the cart.

### Shopping Cart

Customers can add products to the cart, update quantity, change selected product options, remove items, and see cart quantity counts on desktop and mobile views.

Use:

- Gives customers control over their purchase before checkout.
- Supports both simple products and products with options.
- Makes mobile shopping easier.

### Checkout

Customers can place orders with name, phone number, address, delivery area, order note, shipping charge, and payment method. The system supports both logged-in customers and customers who order without a previous account.

Use:

- Keeps ordering simple for first-time buyers.
- Allows repeat customers to use their saved account.
- Collects the delivery details needed to process the order.
- Supports cash on delivery and online payment options.

### Payment Options

The project includes support for cash on delivery, bKash, and ShurjoPay payment flows.

Use:

- Gives customers flexible ways to pay.
- Supports local payment behavior in Bangladesh.
- Helps the business accept both manual and online payments.

### Order Confirmation

After checkout, customers are taken to an order success page. The system can also send order-related messages through configured communication tools.

Use:

- Confirms that the order was received.
- Reduces customer uncertainty after placing an order.
- Gives the business a more professional post-purchase flow.

### Customer Account

Customers can register, log in, view their account, update profile information, change password, and manage basic account details.

Use:

- Helps repeat customers access their history and profile.
- Makes future orders easier.
- Gives the business a customer database for service and follow-up.

### Customer Order History and Invoice

Logged-in customers can view their previous orders, open invoices, and read order notes.

Use:

- Gives customers transparency about their purchases.
- Reduces support requests about order information.
- Provides a clear record for both customer and business.

### Order Tracking

Customers can track orders using phone number and invoice/order information.

Use:

- Lets customers check order progress without contacting support.
- Improves trust after purchase.
- Reduces workload for the customer service team.

### Product Reviews

Customers can submit product reviews and ratings. Reviews can be reviewed by the admin before being shown publicly.

Use:

- Builds trust for future buyers.
- Gives the business feedback on products.

Use:

## 9. Typical Business Workflow

1. Admin adds product categories, brands, sizes, colors, and products.
2. Admin uploads banners and selects hot deals, flash sales, or campaign products.
3. Customer visits the website, browses products, searches, or opens a campaign page.
4. Customer adds products to cart and places an order through checkout.
5. Admin receives the order in the admin panel.
6. Admin reviews the customer and order details, updates status, and prepares delivery.
7. Admin can send the order to courier if the courier service is configured.
8. Customer can track the order and later submit a review.
9. Management reviews inventory, profit, stock, and order reports.

## 10. Business Benefits

- Centralizes online sales, customer records, orders, product catalog, and inventory.
- Reduces manual work for order processing and delivery preparation.
- Improves customer trust with reviews, order tracking, clear product details, and contact options.
- Supports marketing campaigns through banners, hot deals, flash sales, landing pages, pixels, and tracking tools.
- Helps management make better decisions through dashboards, reports, inventory views, and profit calculations.
- Supports future expansion through customer accounts, app-ready content feeds, configurable integrations, and staff permission controls.

## 11. Suggested Client Handover Notes

Before using the project live, the client should prepare:

- Final business name, logos, favicon, and social sharing image.
- Product categories, product photos, product descriptions, prices, and stock numbers.
- Delivery areas and shipping charges.
- Payment account details for enabled payment methods.
- Courier account details for enabled courier services.
- SMS and email sending account details.
- Website policies such as return policy, privacy policy, terms, and order policy.
- Marketing tracking codes such as Facebook Pixel, Google Tag Manager, or verification codes.

This preparation will help the website operate smoothly after launch and will make the admin panel easier for the business team to use.
